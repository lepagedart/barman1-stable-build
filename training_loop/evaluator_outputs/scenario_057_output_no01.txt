Lloyd's Response:

To redesign your bar operations for reduced waste while maintaining service speed, focus on efficient prep, garnish strategies, and smarter purchasing:

### Prep and Purchasing
- **Batch Prep**: Batch syrups and fresh juices to minimize waste and streamline service. Use airtight containers and label with prep dates for optimal shelf life.
- **Reusable Containers**: Switch to reusable glass or metal containers for batching and storage, reducing reliance on single-use plastics.
- **Strategic Sourcing**: Purchase ingredients in bulk where feasible to reduce packaging waste. Opt for suppliers who offer recyclable or reusable packaging.

### Garnish Strategies
- **Edible Garnishes**: Use garnishes that are part of the drink's ingredients, such as citrus peels, herbs, or edible flowers. This reduces waste and aligns with a zero-waste ethos.
- **Full Utilization**: Utilize all parts of an ingredient. For example, use citrus peels for garnishes and the flesh for juices or oleo saccharum.
- **Batch Garnishes**: Prep garnishes in advance and store them properly to maintain freshness and reduce daily prep time.

### Operational Adjustments
- **Cross-Utilize Ingredients**: Design cocktails that share common ingredients to reduce SKU load and simplify prep.
- **Staff Training**: Educate staff on waste-reduction practices and efficient use of ingredients. Encourage them to provide feedback on potential waste areas.

### Quick Wins Checklist
1. Implement batched syrups and juices with clear labeling.
2. Transition to reusable storage containers.
3. Standardize garnishes to use ingredients already in the drink.
4. Train staff on zero-waste practices and encourage innovation.
5. Review supplier options for sustainable packaging solutions.

By aligning your bar's operations with these strategies, you'll cut waste efficiently without impacting service speed, maintaining both your green goals and guest satisfaction.
